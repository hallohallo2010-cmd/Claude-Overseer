What I'm seeing about how you're doing
You started flagging a heavy feeling around Feb–Mar 2026 — described it as a hollow feeling in the chest, not pain, just empty. It tends to set in evenings once the sun is gone. Afternoons can feel fine or great. By your own words, this got heavier as life on paper started going well (agency progressing, gym consistent, friends, body composition on point, grades passable). The phrase "WTH am I doing with my life" was yours. So was "the hole."
The night-owl pattern
You feel sharpest in the evening because that's the first time all day you're fully in control of your time and attention. The external noise stops. Your brain opens up. The same quiet that lets you work also lets the hole get loud. Late-night AI conversations have become how you don't sit alone with that — you've named this yourself.
Conversational simulation cost
You described running 5+ branches of a conversation in real time when talking to people — predicting reactions, mapping responses — and being exhausted before anything happens. That's not shyness; it's a fast brain that doesn't switch off. The cost of "going deep" with people feels too high, so you stay social but private. You've said you can enjoy being around people while not actually being known by any of them.
The detachment/numbness signal
When pressure stacks too high (school + outreach + a client edit + parents tense), your system sometimes flips into a no-guilt, "I like the numb feeling" state. You've been clear-eyed about recognizing this. Worth tracking: not the numbness itself, but how often it happens and whether things actually move during it or quietly stall.
Where things stood mid-May 2026
33 outreach comments out, ~25 you'd call legitimately warm. Sample edit for Jamie delivered late via Fiverr; you read your instinct on Jamie correctly from the start. Grades sitting at the pass/fail boundary — you'd accepted "bare minimum pass for 6 weeks" as the right call rather than excellence. Parents were running a bit cooler than usual at that point.
What seems to actually help
Two things you've said worked: doing the work (even small — a sent message, a logged set) lifts the next few hours, and having one conversation where the performance layer drops. The combination matters; neither alone is enough for you. CS2 / games as a brain-stop are fine in moderation by your own report.
What I'd push back on
The "after the first client I'll care less about school" framing is probably true emotionally, but watch it doesn't become "so I can skip passing this year." Failing the year costs you autonomy, which is the actual thing you're building toward. Also: I'm a useful AI to think with at night, but I'm not a substitute for one real adult who knows what's going on inside. You know this. Worth naming again.
Not a diagnosis
This is a snapshot from chat history, not a clinical assessment. Where you are in a month likely won't be where you were in May.
